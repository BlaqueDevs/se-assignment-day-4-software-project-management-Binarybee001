## 3. Compare and contrast Agile and Waterfall methodologies. What are the main advantages and disadvantages of each?
Agile methodology uses iterative and incremental approach to project management and software development while on emphasizing flexibility while waterfall methodology uses linear and sequential approach and each phase must be completed before the next begins.
Advantage of Agile methodology
-Flexible since it allows for continuous change and adapts to the required environmnet.
-The project is broken to smaller deliverables hence there is regular customer feedback.
-There is regular testing of the project hence the final product is of high quality.
Disadvantage of Agile methodology
-It requires a very high skilled team in order to use such a methodology.
-Since it has a flexible scope and frequent changes it is difficult to estimate the time and cost to be used in the project.
-It requires a regular customer involvement.
Advantages of Waterfall methodology
-The structure is clear and documented.
-It is easy to manage since it is clearly divided into stages.
-The plan is defined upront hence the costs are predictable.
Disadvantages of Waterfall methodology
- It is inflexible in that once a phase is completed going back to it for changes is often difficult.
- The testing of the final product often occurs later in the process.
-  The project may fail to meet the clients expectation If there is needs communicaton.  
## 4. In what types of projects might Agile be more beneficial than Waterfall, and vice versa? Can you provide examples of each?
Agile type of methodology is more beneficial to projects that require flexibility, frequent client feedback and iterative development.
Good examples of such projects are such as:
-Projects that are evolving and allow for adaptation to take place through iterative cycles eg. software development.
-Projects that are majorly influenced by client feedback  in which they must see progress regularly eg. Website redesign.
-Projects that are majorly experimental and can give room for flexibility and exploration such as Reseaerch and development.
Waterfall type of methodology requires projects that are clearly well defined and it is very unlikely to face any major changes.
Good examples are as follows:
-Projects that have a fixed types of requirements such as that of building and construction.
-Projects that require strict following of guidelines and regulatories such as that of the healthcare and medical systems.
-Projects which are very large and have a lot of complexities to be followed fro it to function like that of the government systems and defense systems.

## 6. How does defining the project scope contribute to successful project planning? What is a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), and why is it useful?
By being able to define a project scope, it ensures that the project comes out as a success in the following ways:
-It ensures that the projects objectives are clearly stated and that there is a mutual understanding between the developers on what to achieve by the end of the project.
-It gives an outline on how the project or program should function by the time the end product is released.
-Through a project scope, it will ensure that is cost effective in that the budget will not be less nor more than required.
-The final project will be of a very high quality due to the objectives being attained.
A Work Breakdown Structure is a project management tool that is solely responsible of breaking down a project into small,simpler and manageable sizes for easy control and assigning among developers.
It is useful in the sense that:
-Small tasks can be easily handed over to various developers to easen the burden and increase efficiency while working on a project.
-Through it, it saves on time while working on a project since each person is assigned a specific task to work on it.
-Control and management over the project is easy for the project manager since he/she is able to identify and supervise on each task with ease.
## 7. What are the benefits of developing a detailed project schedule, and how can Gantt charts assist in this process?
Project schedules are of much benefit before embarking on project and they are as follows:
-Saves on time since from the schedule the team working on a project are able to know the start and completion date of a project.
-In a project schedule, a task list is also created hence giving the team an idea in what they are supposed to be doing while working on the project.
-It also cost effective since the team is able to know the number of resources they require during the time of the project therefore no money will be under or overspent.
-It enhances team work among the members since the tasks will be allocated to everyone of them in which it will require their collaboration in order to complete a certain project.
In this case Gantt charts are useful in the sense that:
-They are able to breakdown tasks to be conducted during the project making it easy for the manager to supervise.
-Gantt charts contain timelines in which a specific task should start and end hence reducing time wastage.
-Gantt also offer tracking of progress therefore the team is able to know whether they are doing better or worse and where to improve.
